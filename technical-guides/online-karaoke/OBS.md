# OBS Studio: Role and Configuration Guide

In this system, **OBS (Open Broadcaster Software)** serves as your digital audio capture engine, screen capture utility, and recording station. Instead of using it to stream video to a platform like Twitch or YouTube, OBS is leveraged here for its powerful internal audio routing and high-quality local recording capabilities.

---

## 1. The Role of OBS in this Setup

When used for online karaoke sessions, OBS plays three critical roles:

* **The Ultimate Audio/Video Copy Tool:** OBS is used to **COPY** the audio or screen output of *any* application on your computer. This gives you complete freedom to use any application—whether it is a web browser playing YouTube karaoke videos with lyrics, a standalone music player, or a specialized app—as your source for the backing tracks.
* **Non-Intercepting Passive Capture:** It is crucial to understand that OBS only *copies* the output of your applications; **it does not intercept or block the output**. This means all applications on your computer will still send their sound to their original default destinations, which is usually your computer speakers or your plugged-in headphones. 
* **The Double-Audio Warning:** Because OBS doesn't intercept the sound, the original application is playing music directly to your default playback device while OBS is simultaneously making a copy of that music. If you configure your setup to also listen to the monitored output of OBS through those same headphones, **you will hear the music twice** (creating a delayed, overlapping echo).
* **Isolating and Routing Audio:** By default, OBS captures all music directly from your computer's master audio system. However, **we want to turn that default behavior off** so that OBS strictly captures your designated music source, and completely ignores background noise or sounds generated by other applications on your computer.
* **Archiving Sessions (The Recording Feature):** OBS has a built-in recording engine. If a club officer wants to preserve an online karaoke session, OBS can be used to locally record both the music screen (showing the lyrics video) and the actual singing audio performed by the group.

---

## 2. Installation: Critical First Steps

When you install OBS for the first time, an **Auto-Configuration Wizard** will automatically launch and prompt you with a question about your primary usage intent:

1. It will ask whether you plan to use OBS primarily to **stream** or to **record**.
2. **If you are a club officer who intends to archive sessions, select the "Optimize just for recording, I will not be streaming" option.**
3. In general, because external software handles the internet transmission, we are *not* using OBS to stream to external video platforms. Therefore, **selecting the recording option is always the safer and more optimized choice** for this setup.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Configuration in OBS

To properly isolate your audio and capture only what you intend, follow these configuration steps in order:

### Step 1: Configure Global Audio Settings (Turn Off Automatic Capture)
Before adding specific elements, you must stop OBS from automatically listening to your whole computer.
1. Go to **Settings** in the bottom-right corner of OBS, and click on the **Audio** tab on the left menu.
2. Locate the **Global Audio Devices** section.
3. **Set all devices in this section (Desktop Audio, Mic/Auxiliary Audio, etc.) to DISABLED.** This ensures OBS does not automatically capture sound sources that you do not want on your recording or stream.

### Step 2: Set the Monitoring Device (The Routing Handshake)
While still inside the **Settings > Audio** menu, you must tell OBS where to send the copied audio:
1. Scroll down to the **Advanced** section and locate **Monitoring Device**.
2. Set this device to the virtual audio pipe or designated digital channel where you are outputting this copied music. (The exact device chosen here will depend entirely on the rest of your setup).
3. Click **Apply** and **OK** to close settings.

### Step 3: Define Your "Scene"
Before you can add any inputs, you must look at the **Scenes** dock at the top left of the OBS window. 
* **What is a Scene?** An OBS Scene is a distinct template or canvas that houses a specific collection of audio and video inputs. 
* **Multiple Scenes:** You can create and save multiple different Scenes. This allows you to rapidly switch between different configurations depending on the activity.
* **Action:** There will be a default item named "Scene". Right-click it, select **Rename**, and change it to something identifiable like `Karaoke Music Source`.

### Step 4: Add Your Sources to the Scene
Now that your scene is created, look at the **Sources** dock immediately below the **Scenes**. 
1. Click the **`+` (Add)** icon.
2. Select **Application Audio Capture** or **macOS Audio Capture(on Macs)**. *(Note: We always use Application Audio Capture for this setup to isolate the music app).*
3. Name it something descriptive, like `Browser Music`. 
4. In the properties window that pops up, select the target window of your music source (e.g., Chrome playing YouTube) from the dropdown list to lock it in.
5. This portion can differ between Windows and Mac. Feel free to experiment. But fundamentally you're trying to capture sound and/or screen from a particular application.

### Step 5: Configure the Audio Mixer and Advanced Routing
Look at the **Audio Mixer** window in the bottom center of OBS. 

> 💡 **The Audio Mixer Rule:** Everything visible in the Audio Mixer window is what is currently being "copied" by OBS in that active Scene. If you switch to a different Scene, the Audio Mixer will instantly change to show a different set of captured inputs.

To route your copied music track out to the rest of your system:
1. Click the **three dots (Gear icon)** next to your source in the Audio Mixer and select **Advanced Audio Properties**.
2. Find your music source in the list and locate the **Audio Monitoring** column.
3. Change the setting from *Monitor Off* to **Monitor Only**, if you're only singing. If you're also recording, set it to **Monitor and Output**.

---

## 4. Understanding Audio Monitoring Modes

Inside the **Advanced Audio Properties** window, the setting you choose in the **Audio Monitoring** dropdown dictates how OBS splits your copied audio data. 

### 🎚️ Monitor Only (Mute Output)
When a source is set to **Monitor Only**, OBS routes the audio data *exclusively* to your designated monitoring device (the virtual audio pipe). 
* **What it does:** It functions as a direct split out of OBS into your external setup, but it completely cuts off the signal from OBS's internal recording engine. 
* **The Result:** The audience or external programs linked to your monitoring device can hear the audio perfectly in real time, but **OBS will not record it**. If you click "Start Recording" while using this mode, the resulting video file will be completely silent on this track.

### 🎛️ Monitor and Output
When a source is set to **Monitor and Output**, OBS duplicates the audio signal and sends it down **two completely separate paths simultaneously**.
* **Path 1 (Monitor):** A copy of the audio is sent out to your designated monitoring device so external apps or routes can receive it.
* **Path 2 (Output):** A second, identical copy of the audio is sent straight into OBS's internal recording pipeline.
* **The Result:** Your external audio setup receives the live music copy via the monitor track, **and** OBS successfully captures and saves that background music to your local video file when a club officer hits "Start Recording". 

### 📊 Mode Comparison Matrix

| Monitoring Setting | Sent to Monitoring Device? | Saved to OBS Video Record File? | Main Purpose in This Setup |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Monitor Only** | **YES** | **NO** | Live routing where an archive recording is *not* desired. |
| **Monitor and Output** | **YES** | **YES** | **Standard Karaoke Mode.** Safely routes music to your system while cleanly recording lyrics and track audio to the club archive. |

## 5. Recording the Group Audio and Video Layout

To compile a complete visual and auditory archive of the karaoke event, you must capture the voices of the group singing along alongside the synchronized lyrics video.

### Step-by-Step Recording Scene Setup
1. Go to the **Scenes** dock, click the **`+` (Add)** icon, and create a separate scene named `Session Recording Master`.
2. **Capture the Group Audio:** In the **Sources** dock for this scene, click the **`+`** icon and select **Application Audio Capture**. Target your group audio software application (e.g., SonoBus) to pull the incoming group vocals into the OBS mixer.
3. **Configure Group Audio Routing:** Open the **Advanced Audio Properties** for this group source. Set it to **Monitor Off** (or *Output Only*). Since you can already hear your group via your system's normal playback, choosing *Monitor Off* here prevents it from going to the OBS output while allowing OBS to record them.
4. **Capture the Visual Lyrics Video:** Click the **`+`** icon in the Sources dock again, and select **Window Capture** (or *Browser* or *macOS Screen Capture*). Target the application window you are using to synchronize the session—such as a browser running **WatchTogether**, which tracks and syncs the YouTube karaoke video playing the lyrics. Stretch this window layer to fit the canvas screen. Note that it's possible that this video capture will also capture the audio. If this is the case, simply mute the source.

### 🎬 The End Result
By configuring your recording scene this way, hitting **Start Recording** packages a complete video archive. The final output file will visually display the YouTube karaoke video with the lyrics moving along in perfect lockstep on screen, perfectly mixed with the synchronized live singing of your online group.

### ⚡ Why the Choice of Group Audio Software Matters (The Latency Factor)
When selecting software to run the live session, latency (audio lag) is the single most important factor:

* **SonoBus (The Recommended Standard):** This setup utilizes SonoBus because it is explicitly designed for high-fidelity, peer-to-peer, ultra-low-latency audio transmission through the internet. This allows the group to sing together in near-perfect synchronization, resulting in a cohesive, clean archive recording.
* **Discord & Zoom (High-Lag Risks):** While you *can* use OBS to capture a Discord voice channel or a Zoom meeting using the exact same steps above, **these platforms are optimized for speech compression, not live music**. They introduce noticeable internet lag and processing delays. Singing over them will cause the group's vocals to arrive heavily out of sync with the backing tracks, destroying the quality of the recorded session.

---

## 6. Summary of Benefits

* **Zero Background Noise:** Because all Global Audio Devices are disabled and your source uses *Application Audio Capture*, unexpected noises (like system error sounds, emails popping up, or private message notifications) will never be copied or heard by the audience.
* **High-Quality Archives:** Club officers can hit the **Start Recording** button in OBS at any time. OBS will cleanly capture the exact scene layout (video lyrics + captured singing audio) and package them into a high-quality video file perfect for the club archives.
